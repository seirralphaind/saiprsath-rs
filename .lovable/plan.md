
# Personal Developer Portfolio Website

## Overview
A modern, dark-themed professional portfolio website for a mid-level backend and cloud engineer. The design prioritizes clarity, credibility, and technical depth to impress recruiters and senior engineers hiring for backend, Java, cloud, or data engineering roles.

---

## Design System
- **Theme**: Dark mode with subtle accent color (blue or teal for a professional, technical feel)
- **Typography**: Clean, modern sans-serif fonts with excellent readability
- **Spacing**: Generous whitespace for a premium, uncluttered look
- **Responsive**: Fully mobile-optimized with smooth scroll navigation

---

## Website Sections

### 1. Hero / Landing Section
- Your professional photo prominently displayed
- Your name and title (e.g., "Backend & Cloud Engineer")
- Strong one-liner value proposition highlighting backend engineering and cloud-native expertise
- Call-to-action buttons: "View Projects" and "Get in Touch"
- Subtle animated background or accent element to add polish without distraction

### 2. About Section
- Brief professional narrative emphasizing engineering mindset and systems thinking
- Highlight your approach: building reliable, scalable, cost-efficient systems
- 2-3 key stats or highlights (years of experience, systems designed, etc.)

### 3. Skills Section (Grouped Categories)
Organized into clear categories with visual skill indicators:
- **Backend**: Java 8/17, Spring Boot, REST APIs, Microservices
- **Cloud (AWS)**: Lambda, IAM, S3, Kinesis, Firehose, Athena, Glue
- **Databases**: DynamoDB, MongoDB, MySQL
- **Frontend**: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Flutter
- **Tools & Practices**: API Design, Event-Driven Architecture, CI/CD

### 4. Projects Section (2-3 Featured Projects)
Each project card includes:
- Project title and summary
- The problem solved and real-world impact
- Architecture overview and AWS services used
- Scalability decisions and trade-offs made
- Tech stack badges

### 5. Architectural Thinking Section
A section showcasing your engineering philosophy:
- **API Design**: RESTful best practices, versioning, documentation
- **Event-Driven Systems**: Kinesis, async processing, decoupled architectures
- **Cost-Efficient AWS**: Right-sizing, serverless patterns, data lifecycle management
- Presented as insightful bullet points or short case studies

### 6. Resume Section
- Brief statement about your experience
- Prominent download button for your resume PDF
- Clean, simple layout focused on the action

### 7. Contact Section
- Email contact with a simple contact form or direct email link
- Clear call-to-action to reach out for opportunities
- Footer with copyright and optional social links for future expansion
